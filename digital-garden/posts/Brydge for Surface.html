Brydge for Surface

2022.08.05

TL;DR: Overall experience of the Brydge is great. Keyboard and trackpad feel a little cheap compared to Type Cover, but is worth sacrificing for a hinge. After using it for a while, I am not concerned about the hinge damaging the display. Just don't chuck it around like a maniac.


I've used the Surface Type Cover since I first got with my Surface Pro 6, so it has seen some wear and tear. Switching to the Brydge 12.3 Pro+ (kinda stupid name) took some getting used to.

The build quality of the Brydge is great. The keyboard layout is pretty much identical to the Type Cover.

# Bluetooth

The Brydge takes a couple of seconds to wake up, which is to be expected for a bluetooth keyboard. Sometimes the trackpad starts lagging but it is able to fix the connection on it's own. Occasionally turning on-and-off the keybaord is needed to fix it.

I try not to have multiple bluetooth devices connected, which usually causes the Brydge's trackpad to lag.

# Hinge

Arguably the most important part of the keyboard, and also the part the I feared the most.

Some users reported that their screen's have shattered due to the hinge design of the Brydge, but I think it won't be a problem unless you put a stupid amount of pressure on the device.

At a certain point when closing the screen, the Brydge collapses on it's own, probably to relieve some of the pressure around the hinge-to-screen area.

# Trackpad

This is an important part of the keyboard, but maybe my least favorite switch from the Type Cover. I was never annoyed by the small trackpad on the Type Cover, so switching to a larger one was nice. The trackpad of the Brydge feels solid, but overall the user-experience is less smooth.

It was one of the first things I noticed, because I've always used very high cursor speed (9 in Window's Settings). The speed was a little different between the two trackpads. It's very small, but just something I've grown used to on the Type Cover.

# Weight

Adds a lot of weight to the overall package. You notice it everytime you pick it up.



Recommendation: Don't buy if you don't have a Surface device (duh)
A really unecessary buy unless you really want a true laptop experience for the Surface Pro/Go/X and have a couple of bucks to spend.

I think I'll probably keep using the Brydge because my Type Cover is in 'eh' condition.

PS. I got the Brydge for quite a discount (over 50% off), so it might be discontinuing or something. Wouldn't recommend paying too much for it.
